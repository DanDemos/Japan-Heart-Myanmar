Medical Massage Development related other activities

Presentation at WBUAP 14th Regional Assembly, Massage Commission Meeting

Presentation in China on 8th May 2018 at Asia Pacific Massage Commission Network of World Blind Union. Japan Heart participated in The World Blind Union Asia Pacific (WBUAP) Regional Massage Commission, the 14th Regional Massage Seminar held in Fuzhou, China. As only organization which presents about the Myanmar situation, Japan Heart was selected as a speaker to speak under the title with “Blind Massage Situation in Myanmar, How blind people take privilege in Myanmar & How to Protect this right in the future”. This was the opportunity to inform the world about Japan Heart’s activities in blind masseurs training, as well as the committee activity to establish the licensure system in Myanmar with an affirmative action policy. Concerning the increase of massage shops in Myanmar, the session speaks about the importance of creating the friendliest system for the persons with visual impairment and maximise the right of blind people by learning from the other country’s situation.

Japan Excursion Trip

The excursion trip was conducted in the last week of May, 2018 to see how the persons with disabilities are working in Japan. The participants could learn about the opportunity in blind medical massage in Japan where blind people are privileged to work in the area. Particularly, the participants visited National Rehabilitation center in Saitama and Tsukuba Blind School to learn about the curriculum and training system for the blind. In addition, participants could see private companies, Softbank, where persons with visual disability are hired as medical masseur as the company’s health keeper. In addition, they visited different private companies including Japan Airline to see how the persons with disabilities are working in private companies such as being in charge of reservation. The more ideas come to the participants in how disability population could work in the context of Myanmar.

Medical Massage Development related Local activities

Japan Heart also organized to launch Medical Massage Text Books, under management of Standard Curriculum Steering Committee members, including related National Association of persons with disabilities, Schools for the blind, in-charge of medical massage training centers, blind masseurs and authorities in-charge from concern department of disability focal Ministry.

Moreover, Japan Heart was a member of Employment Sub-Committee member of National Committee on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (2018-2019).

Mr. Katsuya Ashida

I am Katsuya Ashida, a masseuse, acupuncturist and moxibustionist. Currently, preparing to open the only massage shop for the visual disability in Bangladesh.

From 2012 to 2022, I was dispatched to Myanmar from Heart Medical Group in Japan and was in charge of the operation of Blind Medical Massage “Nagomi”. From 2014 to 2022, I was in charge of education at MMTTC operated by Japan Heart. What I taught at MMTTC is the basics of Japanese massage.

I took care of the basics at the shop and at school. This is because it is more acceptable for patients to perfect basic techniques that are used almost all the time for almost all patients than advanced techniques that are used for only 10 minutes for 1% of patients. If you can do even basic techniques well, the treatment will be comfortable, your muscles will become softer, and it will be easier.

It is important to understand what the patient wants and to tailor the treatment accordingly. It will increase the smiles and happiness of the patient, and also increase the smiles and happiness of those connected to the patient. I believe that smiles and happiness will spread.

Being a masseuse is a job that makes it easy to do that, but I think this applies to any job.

I pray that there will be more smiles and happiness in Myanmar.

Thank you.
