Japan Heart's Mission

Providing medical care to areas where it is currently out of reach, wherever that may be

Supporting the liver transplant surgery of the patient Maung Okka Kyaw
who had suffered from chronic liver disease

As a pioneer for the liver transplant surgery, from (13-3-2017) to (18-3-2017) and from (29-10-2017 to (3-11-2017), professors and specialist doctors teams including pediatric surgeons, anesthesiologists and nurses from Yangon Children's Hospital along with the hospital director were invited for study tours to Kyushu University Hospital in Japan and learned about practical teaching. Maung Okka Kyaw, a 3-year-old boy from Yangon, is a child patient who needed liver transplant treatment due to a condition known as primary billiary atresia that occurs in children. Then with the permission of the director general and director of the Department of Medical Services, Nay Pyi Taw Japan, Kyushu University Hospital in Kyushu City of Japan, the team led by Professor Dr Taguchi successfully performed liver transplant surgery on liver disease patient Maung Ukka Kyaw and liver donor U Lin Tin (father) on March 20, 2018. The hospital director of Yangon Children's Hospital and a team of specialist professors and doctors also went to Japan to join the operation. Now, it has been 6 years since the surgery and Maung Okkar Kyaw is also living happily with his family in Yangon.

It’s a pleasure to meet Japan Heart ❤️

Ma Hnin Thadar Phyu, a 2-year-old child patient who came from a remote area of ​​Yangon, was a 2-year-old child patient who had a colostomy (used as a stool outlet instead of an anus) in her abdomen. A medical condition that made it difficult for her to pass stool easily as a newborn required surgery to create an artificial anus a few days after birth. Two years have passed since then, and this time she needed a surgery to close the hole in her tummy, but due to the difficult conditions in Myanmar, it was not easy to find a hospital that could perform the surgery. Later, her parents got contact of Japan Heart and arrived at Wachet Hospital to receive operation. It was difficult for a 2-year-old as there were food restrictions in the days after surgery, but she really worked hard to overcome it. Little Hnin, who likes to talk and to stay pretty, cried sometimes when she was first admitted at the hospital, but soon became familiar to the staffs and became good friends. Amidst the complicated work at the hospital, little Hnin’s way of speaking and cute smile are a cup of medicine for the teachers at the hospital. Little Hnin has now been discharged from the hospital, healthy and happy.

Achievements of MMTTC Alumni Yar Moe Myint

In the 2015-2016 academic year, I had the opportunity to attend a 1-year course in medical massage taught by Japan Heart and worked as a massage therapist. The youngest son, born to farmer parents in Wa Khe Ma, Irrawaddy, was born blind, so I was given special care by my parents. Due to the family’s economic situation and my parents’ weak views on the blind, I was unable to study at a school. At the age of 6, I went to study at private School for the Blind in Yangon, but I was unhappy and returned home. At the age of 22, despite my parents’ unwillingness, I decided to leave home to learn massage to get a job on my own, and because I had never been to school, I had many difficulties in learning, but as a result of my passion, teaching support and hard work, I reached the life of a massage therapist I envisioned. When I started working at a blind massage shop, I had to continue to learn how to manage human relationship, to be a part of good working environment and skill up my massage treatment techniques for many years to be able to give the best treatment services. I am now happily working in a private massage shop in Hlaing Township with my wife, from Meikhtila, Ma Ei Ei Phyo, who graduated from massage school in 2014-2015. Now, I have established my own family and supported my parents’ medical expenses with my own income. I have the opportunity to be a parent who can raise my 4-year-old son. Being able to benefit the patients around me with massage therapy, I am satisfied with my life. I am also trying to open my own shop one day.

Achievements of MMTTC Alumni Lamai Jasan

From May 2010 to 2011, the medical massage therapist course opened by Japan Heart, was taught directly by Japanese teacher Mr. Shinya Shiozaki, I was able to learn both the manual and practical subjects without difficulty with the translation support of Myanmar staff interpreters. In 2011-2012, it was fun and interesting to practice teaching to two students at Myitkyina School for the Blind, the mother school of mine, and to visit excursion field trips to other schools for the blind in different regions every 3 months. Local knowledge was also gained. In 2012, I received a teacher’s certificate and taught as an assistant teacher. In November 2013, I passed the entrance exam of the Japanese Medical Massage, and from March 2014 to March 2017, I had the opportunity to study and represent Myanmar at Tsukuba University’s blind massage training school for 3 years. Although there were language difficulties, thanks to the help of teachers and Japanese friends, I was able achieve the same marks as other students and passed the Japanese government’s massage therapist licensing exam. After returning to Myanmar, I have been working as a teacher since as it’s my childhood dream, at the training school opened by Japan Heart for the blind people to lead independent lives. In the future, as long as I receive support from Japan Heart, I would like to continue working in the development and teaching sector of the blind. In addition, I was able to open the Sakura Sakura massage shop with basic equipment support from Japan Heart, was closed for 2 years during the COVID-19 epidemic, but was able to reopen with the monthly salary support from Japan Heart and the income received from Mobile Massage Customers who trust in me. In the future, I wish to expand and open new massage shops not only for my own benefit but also for the employment opportunities of the blind.

Achievements of MMTTC Alumni U Tin Moe

U Tin Moe attended Japan Heart Medical Massage Teacher Training Course in 2011 – 2012. The reason I studied medical massage is to share it with blind children and to make a living. During the course, I experienced enjoying learning from a Japanese teacher who teaches well and has strict discipline, enjoy learning medical massage subjects, making friends from various schools, obtaining good teaching and learning methods. After completing the course, I feel happy for learning a skill and increasing knowledge. I am glad to learn about the blind massage therapists from Japan and internationally about. We are happy to have the opportunity to teach back massage techniques to blind children in the future. In practice, there are successes and difficulties. As achievements; In 2014, we were able to open a school for the blind named, Ba Wa Thit (New Life in meaning), and teach massage therapy to blind students. Since 2015, I have been able to start teaching therapeutic massage courses based on the Japanese Anma technique, taught by instructor Mr. Shinya Shiozaki from Japan Heart, to about ten blind students. From 2015 to 2023, a total of 70 students were taught therapeutic massage. There were some difficulties in teaching because of the lack of teaching aids, such as a human skeleton that can be touch by hand, made of natural fiber and modal of internal organs. The purpose of starting a school for the blind are to educate blind students in Myanmar, where there are not enough schools for the blind to learn. Since I am blind, I want to make blind people educated. The current teaching situation at the school for the blind is Class literature (Kg to university level), Music (Myanmar music and western music), therapeutic massage, Computer and Android Phone Access Technology, Crafts (making baskets, wallets, Keychain). As other development activities; opening massage shops and raising school funds, School fundraising through musical performances. Challenges facing are the need for funds for the regular operation of school activities, the need to be fully staffed, Acomes of blind people’s life development success; two university matriculation students passed, obtaining jobs for about 50 blind people who have completed therapeutic massage training, about 10 blind people can open their own private massage shops after receiving massage training, about 10 blind massage therapist are involving in finding school fund, being able to trained elite blind athletes, being able to accept and teach by expanding from 10 to 50 students every year, being able to hire 5 to 15 employees. In the future, we intend to; increase the number of students from 50 to 100, to expand and construct buildings that can accommodate about 100 students, to build stadiums, to expand and open school-owned massage shops, and to be able to expand the number of employees.

Activities in Myanmarz

Wachet Hospital and Japan Heart
Wachet Charity Hospital is as an Origin of Japan Heart as medical activities of Japan Heart were established in this hospital since May 2004. It is located in Wachet Village on Sagaing-Mingun Road, Sagaing Division which is in upper central Myanmar. Although the hospital’s capacity is a 25-bedded hospital, it can accommodate up to 200 patients in its busy days. It was initially built for Sangha (Monk) community, however, later changed to a charity hospital for the public. Japan Heart is an international medical volunteer team which is collaborating with this hospital.
Dream Train
Dream Train Child Development Centre was Established on 29th September, 2011 by the help of “Japan Heart” and it is located on No.270/271, Aung Dagun St., Ward 48, North Dagon Township, Yangon, Myanmar. Our expectation is to help and reach out to children who are facing problems with food, clothing, shelter, education, health care and other social problems with a spirit of humanity and compassion without discrimination of any race, religion, blood and skin colour in accordance with the well-known sayings “ HELP THE WEAK”. All the children joining the centre are expected to overcome all the problems of daily necessity of food, clothing, shelter, health and social problems to become independent in life after getting basic education, a suitable vocational training and higher education. There were 28 children at the time of the establishment of the Dream Train Center. At this time, there are (142) students and every year, students who outstanding in academics and sports are emerging. We would like to express our special thanks to the donors from local and foreign who give from all sides every months and every years.
Japan Heart Disability Development Project of Medical Massage Training for the Blind towards Independent Lives and Inclusion in Society
JH aims to work toward providing social assistance to needy groups of disabled persons in Myanmar, JH seeks to build secure and safe circumstances for disable persons and local community, aims to develop sustainable environment where they are living. For the purpose of the Activities, both authority and JH desire work together in the area of persons with disabilities development based on the communities assistance. Furthermore, Medical Massage Teacher Training 2-years course was initiated with ODA fund support through Japanese Embassy of Myanmar for 3 terms, (2010 – 2013) and JH continued to organized Medical Massage Therapist 1-year course until today.
